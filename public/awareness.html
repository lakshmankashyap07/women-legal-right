Awareness Topics

Domestic Violence Awareness

Understanding domestic violence, its signs, and legal protections available.

Domestic violence includes physical, emotional, sexual, and financial abuse. In India, the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 provides comprehensive protection. Victims can seek protection orders, residence orders, and monetary relief.

Signs of domestic violence: Unexplained injuries, isolation from friends/family, controlling behavior, threats, etc.

What to do: Contact helpline 181, file a complaint at nearest police station, seek medical help, contact NGOs like women's helplines.

Workplace Harassment

Know your rights against sexual harassment at workplace and how to seek justice.

The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013 mandates every workplace to have an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC).

What constitutes harassment: Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, sexually colored remarks, showing pornography, etc.

Procedure: File complaint with ICC within 3 months, can approach Local Complaints Committee if dissatisfied.

Cyber Safety for Women

Protect yourself from online threats, cyberstalking, and digital harassment.

Cyber crimes against women include cyberstalking, revenge porn, online harassment, and identity theft. The Information Technology Act, 2000 and IPC sections 354D, 509 deal with these offenses.

Safety tips: Use strong passwords, enable two-factor authentication, be cautious with personal information sharing, report suspicious activities.

Report cyber crimes: File FIR at nearest police station, approach Cyber Crime Investigation Cell, use online portals like cybercrime.gov.in

Dowry Prohibition Act

Understanding dowry laws and protection against dowry harassment and death.

The Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961 prohibits giving or taking dowry. Section 304B IPC deals with dowry death, Section 498A with cruelty related to dowry demands.

Punishments: Imprisonment up to 5 years and fine up to Rs. 15,000 for giving/taking dowry. Life imprisonment for dowry death.

What to do: Report to police, seek protection orders, approach Mahila Thanas, contact women's organizations.

Right to File Zero FIR

Your right to file FIR anywhere in India, regardless of where the crime occurred.

Zero FIR means you can file an FIR at any police station in India, even if the crime didn't occur in that jurisdiction. The police must register the complaint and transfer it to the appropriate station.

Important for women: Especially useful in cases of domestic violence, sexual assault, or when traveling.

Process: Visit any police station, provide details, get FIR number, follow up with concerned police station.

Self-defense and Personal Safety

Learn self-defense techniques and personal safety measures for women.

Self-defense is your right under Section 96-106 IPC. You can use reasonable force to protect yourself.

Basic techniques: Target vulnerable areas (eyes, nose, throat, groin), use everyday objects as weapons, scream for help.

Safety measures: Stay aware of surroundings, avoid isolated areas at night, share location with trusted contacts, learn self-defense classes.

Domestic Violence Act - Complete Guide

▼

The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 is a comprehensive legislation that provides protection to women from all forms of domestic violence.

What is Domestic Violence?

Domestic violence includes:

  • Physical abuse
  • Sexual abuse
  • Verbal and emotional abuse
  • Economic abuse

Legal Remedies Available

Victims can seek:

  • Protection Orders
  • Residence Orders
  • Monetary Relief
  • Custody Orders
  • Compensation Orders

Where to file: Protection Officer, Magistrate's Court, or Police Station

Sexual Harassment at Workplace - Prevention and Redressal

▼

The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace Act, 2013 mandates employers to prevent sexual harassment and provide redressal mechanisms.

Internal Complaints Committee (ICC)

Every workplace with 10+ employees must have an ICC consisting of:

  • Presiding Officer (woman)
  • At least 2 members from employees
  • One member from NGO or association

Complaint Process

1. File complaint within 3 months
2. ICC investigates within 90 days
3. Recommendations binding on employer
4. Appeal to Local Complaints Committee possible

Cyber Crime Laws Protecting Women

▼

Women face various cyber threats including stalking, harassment, and privacy violations. Multiple laws provide protection:

Key Laws:

  • IT Act, 2000: Sections 66A, 66E, 67, 67A
  • IPC Sections: 354D (Stalking), 509 (Insult to modesty)
  • Indecent Representation of Women Act, 1986

Reporting Cyber Crimes:

• File FIR at nearest police station
• Report to Cyber Crime Investigation Cell
• Use online portal: cybercrime.gov.in
• Contact helpline: 1930

Legal Rights During Police Complaints

▼

Understanding your rights when interacting with police during complaints and investigations.

Your Rights:

  • Right to file Zero FIR anywhere
  • Right to be treated with dignity and respect
  • Right to legal representation
  • Right to medical examination
  • Right to protection from secondary victimization

What Police Must Do:

• Register FIR immediately
• Provide copy of FIR
• Conduct investigation promptly
• Keep you informed of progress
• Ensure your safety
