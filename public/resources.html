Domestic Violence Act

Understanding the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005.

The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 provides protection to women from domestic violence. It covers physical, sexual, verbal, emotional, and economic abuse. The Act provides for:

  • Protection orders
  • Residence orders
  • Monetary relief
  • Custody orders
  • Compensation orders

Women can approach Protection Officers, Police, or Courts for immediate relief.

Workplace Harassment Law

Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013.

This Act mandates every employer to constitute an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) to address sexual harassment complaints. Key provisions include:

  • Definition of sexual harassment
  • Formation of ICC
  • Time-bound inquiry process
  • Penalties for false complaints
  • Protection against victimisation

Complaints must be resolved within 90 days.

Dowry Prohibition Act

Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961 and amendments protect women from dowry-related harassment.

The Dowry Prohibition Act prohibits giving or taking dowry. Key features:

  • Dowry defined as property given in connection with marriage
  • Punishment up to 5 years imprisonment and fine
  • Dowry death provisions under IPC Section 304B
  • Criminal breach of trust for dowry articles

Both giving and receiving dowry is illegal.

Right to File Zero FIR

Understanding Zero FIR - File FIR anywhere in India, regardless of jurisdiction.

Zero FIR allows victims to file FIR at any police station in India, even if the crime occurred in another jurisdiction. Key points:

  • Can be filed at any police station
  • Police must register the FIR
  • FIR number starts with '0'
  • Later transferred to appropriate jurisdiction
  • Prevents delay in registering complaints

Especially useful for women traveling or in unfamiliar locations.
